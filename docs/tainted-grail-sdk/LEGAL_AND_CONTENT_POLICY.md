# Legal and Content Policy

## Purpose

This policy protects contributors, users, game rights holders, O3DE, and the project from unlawful or unclear redistribution.

It is project policy, not legal advice.

## Project status

The Tainted Grail Modding Editor and SDK is an independent community project. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by the game's rights holders.

Names, marks, game assets, and other protected material remain the property of their respective rights holders.

## Allowed contributions

Contributions may include:

- original source code and documentation you have the right to license;
- metadata, schemas, identifiers, and observations that may lawfully be shared;
- small synthetic fixtures created specifically for tests;
- screenshots you have the right to share, with personal and proprietary information removed;
- patches or integrations using public, documented APIs and legally obtained tools;
- links and citations to lawful public sources.

## Prohibited contributions

Do not commit or attach:

- game executables, DLLs, assemblies, bundles, archives, or assets;
- ripped models, textures, audio, video, dialogue, maps, or localisation;
- decompiled or reconstructed proprietary source files;
- encryption keys, authentication material, or platform-protected content;
- content obtained from pirated or unauthorised game copies;
- third-party code without a compatible licence and required notices;
- personal data or another person's private files;
- confidential material received under NDA or private access;
- large excerpts of copyrighted documentation or research without permission.

## Diagnostics and decompilation notes

The project may store metadata and original observations about types, members, schemas, and runtime behavior when lawful.

Prefer:

- exact identifiers and signatures needed for interoperability;
- summaries and independently written notes;
- source fingerprints and locators;
- minimal excerpts only when legally permitted and necessary;
- tooling that operates on the user's own lawful installation without redistributing game content.

Do not commit full decompiled classes, reconstructed proprietary source trees, or extracted game data merely because tools can produce them.

## Test fixtures

Fixtures must be:

- synthetic or clearly redistributable;
- minimal;
- free of private paths and personal data;
- documented with provenance and licence when third-party;
- unsuitable for reconstructing protected game content.

Hash values and schemas may be stored without the original protected file when that is sufficient for testing.

## Generated packages

Official project releases must not include game files or private workspace contents.

Generated mod packages are the responsibility of their pack owner. The editor should help identify ownership, dependencies, assets, licences, and release contents but does not certify legal rights.

## Trademarks and naming

Use names and marks only to identify compatibility, target software, or factual subject matter. Do not imply official status, partnership, sponsorship, or endorsement.

Pack authors should choose distinct project branding and include an independence disclaimer when public presentation could cause confusion.

## Third-party dependencies

A dependency requires:

- identifiable source and maintainer;
- licence compatible with repository and distribution goals;
- required notices and attribution;
- security and maintenance review;
- no hidden redistribution of proprietary game content.

## Takedown and disputes

Maintainers may remove or disable content when ownership, licence, privacy, or legal status is unclear.

A person raising a rights concern should identify:

- the material;
- its repository location;
- the claimed right or authority;
- requested action;
- a safe private contact method when details should not be public.

Do not publish personal information in a takedown issue.

## Contributor responsibility

By submitting a contribution, you represent that you have the right to submit it under the repository's licence terms and that required attribution and notices are included.

DCO sign-off is required for commits but does not replace careful content and licence review.
